Part II What Drives BorrowerAPR

by Marah Salahat

Investigation Overview

This presentation explores key factors influencing Borrower APR in Prosper loans, such as credit grade, loan amount, and status. The goal is to highlight how these factors affect interest rates and loan outcomes for borrowers and lenders. Key insights include: higher-risk credit grades lead to elevated APRs, larger loans slightly reduce APRs, and investor-backed loans tend to have lower rates.

Dataset Overview and Executive Summary

The Prosper loans dataset contains ~114,000 loan records with features like BorrowerAPR, LoanOriginalAmount, CreditGrade, LoanStatus, and more. After cleaning (handling missing values, dropping irrelevant columns), we analyzed ~80,000 valid records.

Key Findings:

  1. Borrower APRs are right-skewed, averaging ~0.2 (20%), with most below 0.3.
  2. Credit grade strongly correlates with APRhigh-risk grades (e.g., HR) pay 10-15% more.
  3. Larger loans have lower APRs, but higher monthly payments.
  4. Current/completed loans dominate; charged-off loans have higher APRs.
  5. Investors prefer medium-sized, low-APR loans, suggesting lower risk.

Summary of Findings

Borrower APR is primarily driven by credit grade and loan size, with implications for loan status and investor involvement. High-risk borrowers pay more, but larger loans offer slight relief. Lenders/investors should prioritize credit grade for risk assessment. Future analysis: Predictive modeling for APR based on these factors.
